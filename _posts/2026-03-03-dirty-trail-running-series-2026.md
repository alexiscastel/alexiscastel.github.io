I signed up for seven races in the Dirty Trail Running Series this year. There are actually nine in total, but by the time I found out about it, two had already passed. Seven still felt like a lot when I clicked register. That's basically my whole season. That’s probably why I did it.

![Dirty Trail Running Series graphic for the 2026 season.]({{ '/files/dirty-trail-running-series/dirty-trail-running-series.png' | relative_url }})

I tend to do better when I have something concrete on the calendar. Without that, I either drift or push too hard and end up dealing with small injuries. This gives me structure without having to overthink it. This way, the next race is always in sight.

What I like about this series is how simple it is. It’s local, it’s consistent, and I don’t have to spend time searching for races or planning logistics. With work, training, and a family, that matters more than I expected.

It also happens to fit really well with the two Michigan adventure races I already signed up for. That wasn’t planned, but it makes the whole season feel cohesive.

More importantly, these are trail races. I’ve never been that interested in big, crowded road races. I prefer being out on dirt, dealing with uneven terrain, small climbs, and not really knowing what’s coming next.

Trail races are not a first for me, I have done many over the years, with some amazing ones in France during the summer of 2024. Yet, I haven't done too many around Cleveland. Let's see how this works.

## The Season Plan

Here’s what the year looks like right now:

Seeing it all laid out like this, it’s a lot of racing, but it’s also spread out enough that it feels manageable.

## Why I Care About This

For me, this is about building consistency across the year.

I’m already juggling running, weightlifting, and a bit of rock climbing. This series gives me a backbone for the running side without taking over my life.

I also enjoy the idea of racing regularly enough so that I can actually measure my progress.

## First Race: Flirt with Dirt 10K

The first race I ran was Flirt with Dirt, and I did the 10K.

![Start line scene from Flirt with Dirt.]({{ '/files/dirty-trail-running-series/photo_race_start_line.jpg' | relative_url }})

What stood out immediately is remebering how great racing feels. I often feel stuck/slow when running in town around the house. Racing gives me the extra adrenaline so I push myself and make progress.

The other thing I noticed is how great it feels to go in the early morning in the woods. I got to discover new trails in nature reservations I thought I knew fairly well.

Yet, I have not seen everything. I’m still figuring out what a “good” race even looks like on trails.

![Dirty Trail Running Series race shirt.]({{ '/files/dirty-trail-running-series/photo_race_shirt.jpg' | relative_url }})

## What I've Learned So Far

A few early takeaways:

- Pace matters less than effort on trails
- My cadence drops when the terrain gets technical
- Leg fatigue builds differently than on the road
- Recovery seems more important, even after shorter races

After an intense race, I get an almost all day runner's high. Something that is hard to describe. It also feels almost like I had a great sauna session.

## What I'm Still Figuring Out

The main question I have right now is how to approach each race.

With this many events, it doesn’t make sense to go all out every time. But I also don’t want to just jog through them either. The next one is a half marathon, and I haven't run one of these in a while.

I’m thinking of treating the season as a mix:
- a couple of priority races where I push
- a few where I focus on execution and pacing
- and some that are essentially structured long runs

I’m also still dialing in the basics:
- which shoes actually work best on these courses
- how much fueling I need for trail races
- how to balance all of this with strength training and climbing

## Notes / Gear / Logistics / Lessons

The plan is to keep this simple and update as I go:
- what worked
- what didn’t
- how each race felt compared to expectations

Nothing fancy, just enough to learn from one race to the next.
