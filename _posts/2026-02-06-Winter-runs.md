## Icy Run – Lakewood, OH

Some runs are about pace. This one was more about staying upright.

On a snowy day, I went out for 4.5 miles in Lakewood with a simple goal: keep moving, don't get hurt, and treat the ice with some respect.

- 4.50 miles
- 45:57 moving time
- 10:12/mi average pace
- 28°F, cloudy, windy

With slick patches all over the route, this was never going to be a fast run. That was fine. I shortened the stride, stayed controlled on turns, and focused more on rhythm than speed.

![Route map from the run.]({{ '/files/winter-runs/map.png' | relative_url }})

## Traction Setup

I used [Yaktrax Run traction devices](https://www.rei.com/product/199019/yaktrax-run-traction-device).

I am honestly not sure why they get mixed reviews, because I love them. For this kind of run, they made a big difference. Instead of constantly second-guessing every step, I could just settle in and run.

They also seem to work especially well with my somewhat stiff ASICS GEL SONOMA 7. That shoe is not the lightest or most flexible option I own, but the added structure actually felt helpful here. The whole setup felt stable and predictable, which is exactly what I wanted on ice.

![Yaktrax on my shoes before the run.]({{ '/files/winter-runs/cranpons.jpg' | relative_url }})

## Layers Matter

The other thing that really worked was clothing.

I dressed in layers, and for once I feel like I got it right. I was warm enough to get out the door, but not so bundled up that I overheated once I got moving. That balance matters a lot more in winter than I sometimes admit.

On a run like this, being comfortable is not just about comfort. If you're cold, tense, or distracted, it's harder to move well. The layers helped me stay relaxed and just keep ticking through the miles.

![Post-run selfie after the icy miles.]({{ '/files/winter-runs/selfie.jpg' | relative_url }})

## Takeaways

- Winter pace does not matter much if the footing is sketchy
- Good traction makes the run feel manageable instead of stressful
- The ASICS GEL SONOMA 7 paired surprisingly well with Yaktrax
- Layering well can make a cold run feel far more enjoyable

This was not a big workout and not a breakthrough run. But it was a solid reminder that winter running can still be fun if the goal is simply to move well, stay safe, and adapt to the conditions instead of fighting them.
