Over the last year, I picked up flying DLGs. They are light, efficient, and a lot of fun, but they are also thin composite structures, which means small mishaps can turn into real repairs.

![My DLG before the repair.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/1.jpg' | relative_url }})

This break was entirely on me. I let a couple of kids help retrieve the plane, and the wing was held by the tip instead of by the root. That was enough to crack the structure.

![A flying-day shot of the plane before the repair.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/2.jpg' | relative_url }})

The damage was localized, but it was clearly more than a cosmetic mark.

![Close-up of the cracked wing skin.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/3.jpg' | relative_url }})

I had seen this kind of repair done once before, and my friend Adam taught me the general approach. This time I had to do it on my own, so the first step was confirming the plan and marking the area that needed to be opened up.

![Marked repair area showing the planned work zone.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/4.jpg' | relative_url }})

After that, I opened a groove so I could add a stiffening balsa insert and give the repair some internal structure.

![The wing tip after the initial prep work.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/5.jpg' | relative_url }})

Then I dry-fit the balsa piece before bonding it in place with JB Weld 5-minute epoxy.

![Dry-fitting the balsa insert before bonding.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/6.jpg' | relative_url }})

Once the insert was trimmed and sanded flush, the next job was laying a single ply of carbon over the repair. Cutting and handling thin carbon cloth is the part that still feels the most delicate to me.

![The carbon patch cut and ready for the layup.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/7.jpg' | relative_url }})

I did not stop for enough pictures while the epoxy was live, so I used this shot from the previous repair done by Adam to show the stage where the carbon ply sits under the mylar before bagging.

![Carbon ply under mylar before vacuum bagging.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/8.jpg' | relative_url }})

Then it all went into the bag for the cure.

![The wing sealed in the vacuum bag.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/9.jpg' | relative_url }})

A borrowed vacuum pump kept pressure on the layup, and a heated blanket stayed on overnight to help everything cure cleanly.

![The repair curing under a heated blanket.]({{ '/files/malibu-repair/10.jpg' | relative_url }})

I am a bit nervous about how the ply will settle. The mylar shifted a little and disturbed quite a few fibers, but that is part of learning. Even if the surface still needs a bit more sanding, getting through the full process solo felt like a real milestone.
