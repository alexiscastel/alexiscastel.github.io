## Flirt with Dirt 10k – Brecksville, OH

This was my first race in the Dirty Trail Running Series this year, and a pretty good way to start.

Cold morning, around 28°F, but honestly not a big deal once things got going. The course was classic Brecksville: rolling hills, tight trails, a bit of mud, and just enough chaos to keep it fun.

![Sunrise before the start at Flirt with Dirt.]({{ '/files/flirt-with-dirt/sunrise.jpg' | relative_url }})

### The race

I went in without a super clear plan. Somewhere between “don’t blow up” and “see what happens.”

The start was fast, maybe a bit too fast. First mile at 7:50 pace, which is not exactly conservative for a trail race with elevation. But it felt good, so I let it happen.

Then the course started doing what trail courses do. Up, down, twist, repeat.

The elevation wasn’t extreme, but constant. No real flat sections where you can settle in. You’re always adjusting effort.

![Course map for Flirt with Dirt 10k.]({{ '/files/flirt-with-dirt/course-map.png' | relative_url }})

### The fun part: water crossings

There were four stream crossings.

![Frozen puddle near the course before the race.]({{ '/files/flirt-with-dirt/frozen-puddle.jpg' | relative_url }})

This was also the first run with my brand new ASICS Trabuco 14 GTX. I know, not ideal to race in brand new shoes, but I figured a 10k was short enough to take the risk.

Turned out to be a great call.

Feet stayed dry through all the crossings. No hotspots, no surprises. Honestly forgot about the shoes entirely, which is exactly what you want.

### Mid-race

Miles 2 and 3 slowed down a bit as the climbing stacked up. Nothing dramatic, just the cost of running trails.

Then mile 4 was some form of a turning point. 7:36 pace, somewhere between enjoying some downhill and finding my rythm.

From there it was just about holding on and not doing anything stupid.

### The finish

Finished in **49:24**.

![Official Flirt with Dirt race result graphic.]({{ '/files/flirt-with-dirt/flirt-with-dirt-results.png' | relative_url }})

- 6.00 miles  
- 8:17/mi average pace (Strava)  
- 751 ft elevation  
- 8th overall / 155  
- 2nd in age group (35–39)  

[Strava activity link](https://www.strava.com/activities/17888007868?utm_source=com.strava&utm_medium=referral)

Pretty happy with that.

It felt like a controlled effort, not an all-out race. Which is a good sign this early in the season.

![Post-race selfie with medal at Flirt with Dirt.]({{ '/files/flirt-with-dirt/selfie.jpg' | relative_url }})

### Takeaways

- Trail pacing is still something I need to respect more. I tried to use the Garmin PacePro feature, but didn't exactly follow the plan.
- Effort matters more than pace on this kind of course  
- The Trabuco GTX might be a really solid choice for wet races  
- Starting the series with a good result takes some pressure off  

### What’s next

This is race 1 out of 7 for the series.

The goal isn’t just to show up, but to stay consistent across all of them. No injuries, no big swings, just steady progress.

If this race is any indication, it’s going to be a fun season.
